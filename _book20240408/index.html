Mtg Nov 8, 2023

  • SWAN: Lala is 30% as analyst
  • Data coordinating center at Pittsburg
  • Nenette is one of the PIs of the study
  • Study has been going on since 1990; annual visits (up 16 visits)
  • 3302 women in the sample (at baseline)
  • Surveys collected every year (mostly)
  • Some variables have been modeled as TVC and others as fixed
  • Covariates of interest:
  • AMH is the hormone of interest: long data
  • New way of measuring AMH; can detect below the limit of detection
  • Outcome of interest (time to): Final menstrual period; we don’t’ have that for everyone
  • There is a sample of ~1400 (only about 1k with at least 3 measures of AMH) for whom we have final menstrual period
  • Covariates:
  • BMI, smoking there might be TVC
  • There might be a change point
  • Sample
  • Beginning of the study: enrolling women around 40 premenopausal with 3 regular cycles
  • There is ethnic diversity
  • Menopausal stage; 60 days of menorrea (defines women are in the transition)
  • There might be left truncation
  • Leave 1/3 for testing
  • Future work:
  • Change point
  • Subgroups
  • Including those individuals who did not transition (hormonal treatment)
  • Model below the limit of detect
  • Do you have regular measures of AMH and new assay?
  • There is other measurements TVC of hormones (FSH and estradiol)

Here are a few refs: 1. Rizopolous (2011 Biometrics): this is the first paper on this topic of dynamic prediction. 2. Campbell (2021 BMC). The survival outcome is interval censored but it will provide an idea of what we are trying to do; note that we programmed our likelihood here but the ideas are similar to Rizopolous and the package below. This paper might be easier to follow at first than the Rizopolous (2011 Biometrics). 3. JMBayes package – see vignette and Rizopolous (2016): software paper of JMBayes. As an exercise it’d be helpful for you to work out an example provided by the package or in the website just to see how things work. Then in the next step we can try to apply to our data. https://www.drizopoulos.com/#inaugural_speech https://www.drizopoulos.com/vignettes/dynamic_predictions
